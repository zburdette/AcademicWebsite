Zach Burdette is a PhD candidate in the Security Studies Program at MIT. His dissertation examines how states manage their trade relationships with their security competitors. His other research focuses primarily on the military balance in the Western Pacific. Zach is also an adjunct researcher at the RAND Corporation, where he has worked on projects related to U.S. grand strategy, defense planning, and escalation management. Prior to MIT, he graduated _summa cum laude_ from Georgetown University with a BS in Foreign Service and an MA in Security Studies.
